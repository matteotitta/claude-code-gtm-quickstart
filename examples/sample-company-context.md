## Executive summary

Linear is a project management and issue tracking tool designed for high-performance software teams. Founded in 2019 by Karri Saarinen (ex-Airbnb design lead) and Tuomas Artman (ex-Uber), the company has built a reputation for speed, craft, and opinionated design in a market dominated by Jira and Asana.

Linear has raised $52M total (Series B led by Accel), serves thousands of software teams, and has become a cultural signifier in the startup ecosystem — teams that use Linear signal a certain standard of engineering and product culture.

The company's GTM motion is product-led with a strong word-of-mouth engine. They've invested heavily in design and performance as differentiators rather than feature breadth.

## 3. Product overview

**Core product:** Issue tracking and project management for software teams.

**Key capabilities:**
- Issue tracking with keyboard-first interface (sub-100ms interactions)
- Project cycles with automated workflows
- Roadmapping with project-level views
- Triage system for incoming requests
- Integrations with GitHub, GitLab, Slack, Figma
- Linear Asks (customer feedback collection)

**Positioning:** "Linear is a purpose-built tool for planning and building products." They position against the complexity of Jira and the genericness of Asana by being fast, opinionated, and designed for engineering teams.

## 6. Strengths and weaknesses

- Opinionated design reduces configuration complexity

**Weaknesses:**
- Primarily serves software teams — limited appeal outside engineering
- Feature set narrower than Jira for large enterprise use cases
- Revenue not public — makes competitive pricing analysis harder
- Less established in non-tech industries
- Opinionated design means less flexibility for edge cases
