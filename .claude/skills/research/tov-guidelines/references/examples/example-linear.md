# TOV example: Linear

A worked TOV extraction for **Linear** — built from public-only sources (linear.app, blog, changelog, founder posts on X).

This shows what the `/tov-guidelines` output looks like when applied to a real public company. Use it as a reference for the structure your own output should follow.

## Brand voice spectrum

| Dimension | Position | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Formal ↔ Casual | **Casual but precise** | Blog uses contractions ("we're", "it's"), but every claim is technically exact |
| Reverent ↔ Irreverent | **Slightly irreverent** | Subtle digs at "issue tracking software for the rest of us"; never mocks competitors directly |
| Matter-of-fact ↔ Enthusiastic | **Quietly confident** | No exclamation marks. Major launches announced as "Today we're shipping…" not "We're so excited to announce!!" |
| Plain ↔ Poetic | **Plain with occasional craft prose** | Long-form essays sometimes lean into rhythm ("software shouldn't feel like work"); product copy never does |

## Voice patterns

### Things Linear DOES

- **Short, declarative sentences.** "Linear is a better way to build software." Not "Linear is a revolutionary, AI-powered, next-generation platform that…"
- **Sentence-case headlines.** "Plan and build products" — not "Plan And Build Products."
- **Em dashes with spaces.** Sparingly. Used to create rhythm, not as a tic.
- **Names the product as a noun.** "Linear" is always capitalized when used as the product. Never "the Linear platform" or "Linear's solution."
- **Refers to users as "teams."** Not "customers," not "users." This is a deliberate framing — Linear is a tool teams adopt together, not individuals onboard solo.

### Things Linear DOES NOT

- **No exclamation marks.** Anywhere. Even in launch posts.
- **No corporate buzzwords.** Zero instances of "innovative," "leverage," "synergy," "cutting-edge," "best-in-class," "next-generation," or "disrupt" across the entire blog.
- **No emoji in product copy.** Some emoji on changelog post titles (🚀, 🎉) but never in the body.
- **No "powered by AI" framing.** Even when shipping AI features (Linear Asks, Magic Linking), the framing is the *outcome*, not the technology.
- **No fake urgency.** "Limited time," "act fast," "don't miss out" — never appear.

## Vocabulary

### Words Linear uses often

- **Build** (not "create," not "make"). "Plan and build products."
- **Ship** (not "launch," not "release"). "Ship faster."
- **Quality** (used sincerely; not as "premium")
- **Craft** (load-bearing word — appears throughout the Linear Method)
- **Velocity** (used precisely — not as a synonym for "speed")
- **Issue** (not "ticket," not "task")

### Words Linear avoids

- **Solution** (bare, as in "our solution"). They say "Linear" or "the product."
- **Platform** (in marketing copy). They say "Linear" or "issue tracking."
- **Revolutionize / disrupt / transform** — all absent.
- **Powerful / robust / scalable** — replaced with concrete claims ("plan a year of work in one view").
- **AI-powered** (as a primary descriptor). AI capabilities are described by what they do, not the tech behind them.

## Sentence-level patterns

### Headlines

- 3-7 words typical. Rarely longer.
- Sentence case.
- Verb-first or noun-first; almost never adjective-first.
- Examples (verbatim from linear.app):
  - "Plan and build products"
  - "Issue tracking the way it should be"
  - "Built for high-performance teams"

### Body copy

- 1-2 sentence paragraphs in marketing copy.
- 3-5 sentence paragraphs in long-form blog posts.
- Topic sentence carries the claim; following sentences add evidence or specificity.
- Almost never starts with "We" — leads with the user benefit or the noun being discussed.

### Calls to action

- Single verb. "Get started." "Try Linear." "Read the method."
- Never compound. Never "Click here to learn more about how Linear can…"

## Tone variations by surface

| Surface | Tone | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Marketing site | Confident, terse | "Plan and build products." 4 words, full claim. |
| Changelog | Warm, observational | "Today we're shipping…" then technical detail |

| Customer-facing emails | Direct, brief | No upsells, no salesy framing |
| Founder posts on X | Crafty, slightly performative | Tight one-liners; ships content like product |

## Do's and Don'ts (for your own writing if mirroring this voice)

**DO:**
- Trust short sentences. If the claim is small, the sentence should be small.
- Use specific verbs ("ship," "build," "plan") instead of generic ones ("create," "make," "develop").
- Reference your team as "teams," not "customers" or "users."
- Sentence-case all titles.
- Use em dashes sparingly with spaces.

**DON'T:**
- Add exclamation marks for energy. The sentence has to carry it.
- Reach for buzzwords. They flatten the voice.
- Stack adjectives. "A powerful, scalable, AI-powered platform" reads as marketing copy.
- Pad sentences. "We are excited to announce that…" → "Today we're shipping…"
