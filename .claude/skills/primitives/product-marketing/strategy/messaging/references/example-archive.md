## 1. Description

Archive is an AI-powered social listening and creator discovery platform purpose-built for short-form video that helps consumer brands automatically capture all UGC across TikTok, Instagram (including Stories and Reels), and YouTube Shorts, source and vet creators, automate manual workflows like gifting and usage rights, and prove ROI through automated reporting. Unlike legacy social listening tools designed for text-based monitoring, Archive's AI watches video, listens to audio, and reads text to detect brand mentions even without tags, enabling small marketing teams at fast-growing consumer brands to run creator programs that previously required larger teams or agencies.

**Source**: https://archive.com | **Accessed**: 2025-12-30
**Confidence**: High

## 2. Status quo and alternatives

What customers currently use or do before adopting Archive.

### Screenshots, spreadsheets, and manual scrolling

**Type**: Manual/DIY

Marketing teams manually scroll Instagram and TikTok looking for tagged posts, screenshot or screen-record each individual post, and update spreadsheets with the image/video files and creator stats. When teams want to turn content into ad campaigns, they tell agency partners to check the spreadsheet. This process is error-prone, exhausting, and means teams regularly miss posts, especially Stories that disappear after 24 hours.

- **Key limitation**: Manually hunting for posts, screenshotting Stories, missing mentions — brands report needing to hire full-time employees just to scroll social media.
- **Creates pain point** → Manually hunting for posts and missing mentions (see Section 3)
- **Source**: https://archive.com | **Accessed**: 2025-12-30
- **Confidence**: High

### Google Drive + Excel trackers

**Type**: Manual/DIY

Teams store screenshots in Google Drive folders and track stats, creator details, and campaign information in Excel spreadsheets. This creates information silos where UGC is scattered across DMs, drives, and tools — making it hard to reuse content or tie it to sales performance.

- **Key limitation**: UGC scattered in DMs, drives, and tools; hard to reuse or tie to sales.
- **Creates pain point** → No automated process for turning UGC into ad content (see Section 3)
- **Source**: https://archive.com | **Accessed**: 2025-12-30
- **Confidence**: High

### Tribe Dynamics

**Type**: Direct competitor
**Market category**: Earned media analytics / Influencer measurement

Enterprise social listening platform focused on earned media value tracking. Tribe Dynamics tracks some tagged content but focuses on influencers with 10K+ followers, providing only a small slice of the creator community rather than the full picture from mega-influencers to everyday customers.

- **Key limitation**: Limited to top 50 posts/month and top 50 influencers/month; focuses on influencers with 10K+ followers, missing everyday customers.
- **Creates pain point** → No way to measure program success or prove ROI (see Section 3)
- **Source**: https://archive.com/product/competitor-insights | **Accessed**: 2025-12-30
- **Confidence**: High

### Sprout Social, Sprinklr, Dash Hudson

**Type**: Adjacent alternative
**Market category**: Social media management / Brand analytics

Traditional social media management platforms that track content the brand posts rather than detecting creator content about the brand. These tools excel at scheduling, publishing, and analyzing owned social content but don't track tagged UGC or creator mentions.

- **Key limitation**: Only track content the brand posts; no creator or UGC detection.
- **Source**: https://archive.com/product/competitor-insights | **Accessed**: 2025-12-30
- **Confidence**: High

### CreatorIQ

**Type**: Direct competitor
**Market category**: Enterprise influencer marketing platform

Enterprise influencer marketing platform with creator databases and campaign management. CreatorIQ requires $36K+ commitment for competitive analysis features (BenchmarkIQ), complex enterprise setup, and doesn't provide the automated UGC capture that Archive offers.

- **Key limitation**: Enterprise-only pricing ($36K+), complex setup, limited to creator databases rather than automated UGC capture.
- **Creates pain point** → Spreadsheets and DMs to find creators (see Section 3)
- **Source**: https://archive.com/blog/creatoriq-alternatives | **Accessed**: 2025-12-30
- **Confidence**: High

## 3. Pain points

Problems and frustrations users face that Archive solves.

### Manually hunting for posts and missing mentions

Every day, social media managers spend countless hours manually monitoring and tracking brand UGC — scrolling Instagram and TikTok, screenshotting each post, updating spreadsheets. Posts get missed overnight and on weekends. Stories disappear before anyone sees them. The more popular a brand gets, the more overwhelming this becomes.

- **Caused by** ← Screenshots, spreadsheets, and manual scrolling (see Section 2)
- **Addressed by** → Capture everything automatically 24/7 (see Section 4)

### No way to measure program success or prove ROI

Marketing teams generate significant social attention through gifting and influencer programs, but have no reporting tools to measure success. They can't communicate to executives how they're performing compared to competitors, making it difficult to justify budgets or prove program value.

Brands great at gifting generate floods of UGC but have no automated process for turning that content into polished ad creative. Getting usage rights, managing whitelisting, and sharing with agencies requires manual coordination that delays time-to-ad.

- **Caused by** ← Google Drive + Excel trackers (scattered content) (see Section 2)
