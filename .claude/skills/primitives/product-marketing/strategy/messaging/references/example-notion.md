## 1. Description

Notion is a connected workspace that combines notes, docs, wikis, and project management into a single platform where teams write, plan, and organize work together. Unlike point solutions that fragment information across tools, Notion provides flexible building blocks — pages, databases, and templates — that adapt to any workflow, serving teams of all sizes who want to reduce tool sprawl and create a single source of truth for their organization's knowledge and work.

**Source**: https://notion.so | **Accessed**: 2025-12-30
**Confidence**: High

## 2. Status quo and alternatives

What customers currently use or do before adopting Notion.

### Scattered documents and local files

**Type**: Manual/DIY

Teams store information in a mix of local files, shared drives, email attachments, and random documents with no central organization. Finding information requires asking colleagues "where's that doc?" and knowledge gets lost when people leave or files get buried.

- **Key limitation**: No single source of truth; knowledge trapped in silos.
- **Creates pain point** → "Where's that doc?" — lost information (see Section 3)
- **Source**: https://notion.so | **Accessed**: 2025-12-30
- **Confidence**: High

### Google Docs + Sheets + Slides

**Type**: Legacy tool
**Market category**: Productivity suite / Cloud office

The default productivity suite for many teams. While familiar and collaborative, Google Workspace fragments information across separate apps — docs don't connect to sheets, and there's no unified way to organize, search, or structure knowledge across the suite.

- **Key limitation**: Information fragments across apps that don't connect.
- **Creates pain point** → Tool sprawl drains productivity (see Section 3)
- **Source**: https://notion.so | **Accessed**: 2025-12-30
- **Confidence**: High

### Confluence

**Type**: Direct competitor
**Market category**: Enterprise wiki / Knowledge management

Atlassian's wiki product used by enterprise teams, often alongside Jira. Confluence provides structured documentation but suffers from complex navigation, slow performance, and a steep learning curve that discourages regular updates.

- **Key limitation**: Becomes a graveyard of outdated documentation.
- **Creates pain point** → Documentation becomes a graveyard (see Section 3)
- **Source**: https://notion.so/product/wikis | **Accessed**: 2025-12-30
- **Confidence**: High

### Multiple specialized tools (Asana + Google Docs + Slack channels)

**Type**: Adjacent alternative
**Market category**: Work management + Productivity + Communication (combined)

Teams cobble together separate tools for project management, documentation, and communication. Each tool excels at one thing but creates context-switching overhead and information silos where the same data lives in multiple places.

- **Key limitation**: Tool sprawl creates fragmented context and duplicated information.
- **Creates pain point** → Tool sprawl drains productivity (see Section 3)
