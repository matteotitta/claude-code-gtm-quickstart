# Anti-patterns + gotchas

Common positioning failures and the gotchas that cause skill outputs to drift toward generic.

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

| Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| "Platform for X" alone | Doesn't explain what it does | Add what the platform enables |
| "AI-powered" as differentiator | Everyone says this now | Focus on outcome AI enables |
| "Best in class" | Unsubstantiated claim | Specific, provable differentiator |
| Listing 10+ use cases | Confuses, doesn't position | Lead with 1-2, expand later |
| New category coining | Moves you off known shelf | Modifier on known category |
| Business outcome only | Table stakes, not differentiator | HOW you deliver the outcome |

---

## Gotchas

- **Template-driven positioning**: Fills in framework blanks mechanically without client-specific insight → Positioning statements must reflect actual competitive dynamics, not generic category language.
- **Too many differentiators**: Lists 7+ differentiators instead of focusing on 2-3 that matter most → Rank differentiators by ICP relevance. Primary anchor + 2-3 supporting differentiators max.
- **Binary strategy not justified**: Picks "challenger" or "leader" without evidence → Binary strategy choice must reference market share data, brand awareness, and competitive landscape.
- **Disconnected from ICP language**: Uses internal company language instead of buyer vocabulary → Cross-reference positioning with icp-behavioural output for buyer-side validation.
- **No hero recommendation**: Produces positioning but skips the concrete homepage hero copy recommendation → Hero recommendation bridges strategy to execution. Required output.
