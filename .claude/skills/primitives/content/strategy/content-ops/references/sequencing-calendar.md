## Cascade templates

### Template 1: Newsletter anchor

```
Newsletter deep-dive (anchor — published Thursday)
  │
  ├─ Same day: LinkedIn pull-quote post (best single insight, standalone format)
  ├─ +1 day (Fri): LinkedIn hot take (contrarian angle from the piece)
  ├─ +2 days (Mon): LinkedIn carousel (key framework visualized as slides)
  ├─ +3 days (Tue): YouTube Short (screen-recorded walkthrough of key section)
  └─ +1 week (Thu): LinkedIn follow-up post (response to comments/questions)

Total derivatives: 5
Cascade efficiency: Track % of newsletters generating 4+ derivatives
```

### Template 2: Podcast anchor

```
Podcast episode (anchor — published Wednesday)
  │
  ├─ Same day: LinkedIn quote post (best soundbite, attributed to guest)
  ├─ +1 day (Thu): Newsletter edition (expanded show notes + key insights)
  ├─ +2 days (Fri): LinkedIn clip post (best 60-second moment as native video)
  ├─ +3 days (Mon): LinkedIn carousel (3-5 key takeaways as slides)
  ├─ +4 days (Tue): YouTube Short (counterpoint or surprising moment)
  ├─ +5 days (Wed): LinkedIn text post (your commentary on guest's POV)
  └─ +1 week: 2 quote graphics (for evergreen queue)

Total derivatives: 7-8
Cascade efficiency: Track % of episodes generating 6+ derivatives
```

### Template 3: YouTube anchor

```
YouTube video (anchor — published Friday)
  │
  ├─ -1 day (Thu): LinkedIn teaser post (hook + "full video drops tomorrow")
  ├─ Same day: LinkedIn short clip (best 60-second moment)
  ├─ +2 days (Mon): LinkedIn text post (key insight expanded)
  ├─ +3 days (Tue): Newsletter mention (embed + commentary)
  └─ +1 week: LinkedIn carousel (framework from video as slides)

Total derivatives: 4-5
Cascade efficiency: Track % of videos generating 3+ derivatives
```

### Template 4: Series installment anchor

```
Series installment (e.g., "GTM Debt Part 3" — published Tuesday)
  │
  ├─ Same day: Primary channel post (LinkedIn text or carousel)
  ├─ +1 day: Newsletter excerpt (if series is noteworthy)
  ├─ +2 days: Infographic derivative (key framework as visual)
  └─ After series complete: Compilation post ("The complete GTM Debt series — all 5 parts")

Total derivatives: 2-4 per installment, 1 compilation at series end
```

### Cascade adaptation rules

Derivatives are NOT reposts. Each is native to its channel:

| Channel | Adaptation Rule |
|---------|----------------|
| **LinkedIn text** | Extract one insight, frame as standalone take. Add personal commentary. |
| **LinkedIn carousel** | Visualize the framework. Each slide = one concept. Max 8-10 slides. |
| **LinkedIn video** | Best 60-second clip. Native subtitles. Hook in first 3 seconds. |
| **Newsletter** | Expand with context, examples, and links. Add personal voice. |
| **YouTube** | Full walkthrough or tutorial format. SEO-optimized title. |
| **YouTube Short** | Surprising moment or counterpoint. Under 60 seconds. |
| **X.com** | Condensed thread format. 3-5 tweets max. |
| **Quote graphic** | Single powerful sentence. Clean design. Evergreen. |

## Sunday planning checklist

### Weekly review protocol

Run every Sunday (or last working day of the week). Time: 30-45 minutes.

**Step 1: Review last week's performance (10 min)**
- [ ] Check TWE scores for all published LinkedIn posts
- [ ] Check newsletter open rate and click-through
- [ ] Check YouTube views and retention (if applicable)
- [ ] Identify outperformers (TWE > 2, open rate > 40%, views above baseline)
- [ ] Note underperformers — any pattern? (topic, format, time, series?)

**Step 2: Update series tracker (5 min)**
- [ ] Mark published installments
- [ ] Update TWE averages per series
- [ ] Check for decision triggers (expand, continue, pivot, retire)
- [ ] Add follow-up ideas from audience comments/questions

**Step 3: Plan next week's content (15 min)**
- [ ] Check pillar mix against targets (are we over-indexing on one pillar?)
- [ ] Select content from approved backlog:
  - Priority 1: Series installments in-progress
  - Priority 2: Cascade derivatives from this week's anchor
  - Priority 3: Evergreen queue fillers
  - Priority 4: Timely/reactive content
- [ ] Assign to channel-specific posting slots (see cadence template)
- [ ] Verify cascade timing is correct (anchor before derivatives)

**Step 4: Assign and confirm (5 min)**
- [ ] Tag team members with assignments in pipeline tool
- [ ] Confirm due dates for draft, review, and publish
- [ ] Check buffer depth — do we have 2+ weeks of approved content?
- [ ] Note any blockers (missing design asset, pending approval, etc.)

**Step 5: Document decisions (5 min)**
- [ ] Log any pillar mix adjustments
- [ ] Note series decisions (expand, retire, etc.)
- [ ] Flag gaps for next week's planning

## Backlog management rules

### Three queues

| Queue | Definition | Examples | Shelf Life |
|-------|-----------|---------|------------|
| **Evergreen** | Timeless content, publish anytime | Frameworks, process breakdowns, career reflections | Indefinite |
| **Timely** | Trend-driven, news-jacking, event-related | Algorithm updates, industry news, conference reactions | < 1 week from trigger |
| **Launch** | Tied to product/feature/service release | Feature announcements, case study reveals, pricing changes | Publish on schedule |

### Backlog hygiene

- **Weekly:** Review timely queue — anything stale? Archive it.
- **Monthly:** Review evergreen queue — anything that no longer fits the pillars? Archive it.
- **Quarterly:** Audit full backlog depth. Target: 30+ ideas in evergreen, 5-10 in timely, launch queue populated per roadmap.

### Idea intake

New ideas enter the backlog from:

| Source | How to Capture | Priority Signal |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Customer conversations | Tag "content idea" in CRM notes | Direct from ICP = high |
| Audience comments/DMs | Screenshot or quote in pipeline tool | Engagement signal = high |
| Competitor content | Log URL + your angle in backlog | Differentiation opportunity = medium |
| Industry news/trends | Drop in timely queue with deadline | Time-sensitive = depends |
| Team brainstorms | Weekly or biweekly ideation sessions | Volume play = varies |
| Personal experience | Log immediately (voice memo or note) | Authentic = medium-high |

## Cross-channel coordination protocols

### Channel roles

| Role | Definition | Example Channels |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Lead** | Where anchor content publishes first | Newsletter, Podcast, YouTube |
| **Amplification** | Where derivatives appear | LinkedIn, X.com, YouTube Shorts |
| **Conversion** | Where BOFU content drives action | DMs, email flows, landing pages |

### Stagger rules

1. **Never publish the same insight on two channels the same day** — audiences overlap
2. **Space derivatives 1-2 days apart** — gives each piece its own engagement window
3. **Vary format across channels** — text on LinkedIn, video on YouTube, visual on X.com
4. **Lead channel publishes first** — derivatives reference the anchor, not the other way
5. **Reactive content breaks the stagger** — timely content posts immediately, wherever it fits

### Cross-channel timing matrix

| Day | LinkedIn | Newsletter | YouTube | Podcast |
|-----|---------|-----------|---------|---------|
| Mon | Series installment or cascade derivative | — | — | — |
| Tue | Text post (standalone or cascade) | Optional: publish here | — | — |
| Wed | Engagement day (comments, DMs) | — | Publish if active | Publish if active |
| Thu | Carousel or infographic | Publish (primary day) | — | — |
| Fri | Video clip or reactive post | — | Publish if active | — |
| Sat | — | — | — | — |
| Sun | Planning (no publishing) | — | — | — |

**Adapt this matrix to actual channel selection.** Not all channels will be active for every client.

## Buffer and emergency protocols

### Buffer depth targets

| Team Size | Minimum Buffer | Healthy Buffer | Surplus |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|---------|
| Solo | 1 week ahead | 2 weeks ahead | 3+ weeks |
| Small (2-3) | 2 weeks ahead | 3 weeks ahead | 4+ weeks |
| Full (4+) | 2 weeks ahead | 4 weeks ahead | 6+ weeks |

### When buffer drops below minimum

1. Pause timely queue — focus on getting evergreen content through pipeline
2. Reduce posting frequency temporarily (e.g., LinkedIn 5x → 3x/week)
3. Pull from evergreen backlog — post ready-to-go content
4. Flag in Sunday review — adjust team workload or cadence

### Emergency content slot

Reserve 1 slot per week for reactive/trending content:
- Industry news that demands a take
- Competitor move worth commenting on
- Viral post in your niche worth responding to
- Customer win worth announcing immediately

**Rule:** If no emergency arises, fill the slot from the timely queue or leave it empty. Don't force reactive content.
