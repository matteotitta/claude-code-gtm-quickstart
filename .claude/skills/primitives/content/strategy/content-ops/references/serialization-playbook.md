# Serialization playbook

Reference file for `content-operations` Phase 1: Content Serialization.

---

## Series arc templates

### 1. Problem arc

**Structure:** Identify → Unpack angles → Reveal root cause → Propose shift

**Best for:** TOFU — building awareness of a problem the audience doesn't know they have

**Template:**

| # | Installment Role | Hook Pattern | Example |
|---|-----------------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Name the problem | "Most [audience] don't realize they have [problem]." | "Most B2B SaaS companies have GTM debt. They just don't know it yet." |
| 2 | Angle 1 | "Here's what [problem] looks like in [specific area]." | "Your homepage says 3 different things. That's messaging debt." |
| 3 | Angle 2 | "It gets worse: [problem] also shows up in [area 2]." | "Your last 3 launches had the same CTA. That's launch debt." |
| 4 | Root cause | "The root cause isn't [obvious thing]. It's [deeper thing]." | "GTM debt isn't a content problem. It's a context problem." |
| 5 | The shift | "Here's what [audience] who've solved this do differently." | "Companies that audit their GTM debt quarterly grow 2x faster." |

**Notes:**
- 4-6 installments typical
- Each angle should be independently valuable (someone can read #3 without reading #1)
- The final installment pivots from problem → solution (bridge to MOFU)
- Works best as text posts on LinkedIn, long-form in newsletter

### 2. Mechanism arc

**Structure:** Introduce concept → Layer 1 → Layer 2 → Layer 3 → Full application

**Best for:** MOFU — building expertise and trust through progressive teaching

**Template:**

| # | Installment Role | Hook Pattern | Example |
|---|-----------------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Name the mechanism | "I don't [common approach]. I [your approach]. Here's why." | "I don't start with the brief. I start with the context." |
| 2 | Layer 1 (foundation) | "The first step most people skip: [layer 1]." | "Step 1: Research layer — talking to customers before touching the brief." |
| 3 | Layer 2 (build) | "Once you have [layer 1], you can [layer 2]." | "Step 2: Synthesis — patterns, not templates." |
| 4 | Layer 3 (advanced) | "This is where most frameworks break: [layer 3]." | "Step 3: Application — same framework, different context, different output." |
| 5 | Full application | "Here's the whole mechanism in action: [example]." | "Full walkthrough: how Context Engineering shaped [Client]'s positioning." |

**Notes:**
- 4-5 installments typical
- Progressive complexity — each layer requires the previous
- Callbacks to earlier installments build series loyalty ("In Part 2, I covered X. Here's why that matters now.")
- Works best as carousels/infographics on LinkedIn, tutorials on YouTube

### 3. Case study arc

**Structure:** Context → Challenge → Approach → Results → Lessons

**Best for:** BOFU — proving results with real examples

**Template:**

| # | Installment Role | Hook Pattern | Example |
|---|-----------------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Context | "[Company] had [situation]. Here's what happened." | "Voiceflow had a positioning problem. Their product had outgrown their story." |
| 2 | Challenge | "The real challenge wasn't [obvious]. It was [deeper]." | "The challenge wasn't messaging copy. It was that 3 teams had 3 different stories." |
| 3 | Approach | "Here's exactly what we did (and what we didn't)." | "We ran a 2-week positioning sprint. Here's the day-by-day." |
| 4 | Results | "[Metric]. [Metric]. [Metric]." | "New homepage: 3x conversion. Sales cycle: -40%. Team alignment: finally." |
| 5 | Lessons | "What I'd do differently, and what I'd repeat." | "3 lessons from the Voiceflow sprint that apply to any Series B." |

**Notes:**
- Can be ongoing (each installment is a different company) or single-company deep dive
- Ongoing series: post 1 case study every 2-4 weeks
- Single-company series: 3-5 installments over 2 weeks
- Works best across all channels — LinkedIn posts, newsletter deep-dives, YouTube walkthroughs

### 4. Framework arc

**Structure:** Name the framework → Apply to Scenario A → B → C

**Best for:** MOFU/BOFU — establishing a named methodology

**Template:**

| # | Installment Role | Hook Pattern | Example |
|---|-----------------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Name it | "I call this [framework name]. Here's the premise." | "First Mile / Last Mile: the quality framework I use for every deliverable." |
| 2 | Scenario A | "[Framework] applied to [specific context]." | "First Mile applied to website launches: why the first page matters most." |
| 3 | Scenario B | "[Framework] applied to [different context]." | "Last Mile applied to content: why distribution eats creation." |
| 4 | Scenario C | "[Framework] applied to [surprising context]." | "First Mile applied to pricing: the first number anchors everything." |

**Notes:**
- 3-5 installments typical
- The framework should have a memorable name
- Each scenario application should feel surprising or non-obvious
- Works best as carousels and text posts on LinkedIn

### 5. Opinion arc

**Structure:** Contrarian take → Supporting evidence → Counter-arguments → Resolution

**Best for:** TOFU/MOFU — building thought leadership through provocation

**Template:**

| # | Installment Role | Hook Pattern | Example |
|---|-----------------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Provoke | "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's why." | "Content calendars are a crutch. I'll die on this hill." |
| 2 | Evidence | "Here's the evidence: [data, stories, examples]." | "3 companies I've worked with ditched their calendar. All grew faster." |
| 3 | Counter-argue | "But what about [obvious objection]? Fair point." | "But Titta, consistency matters. Yes — and calendars aren't the only way." |
| 4 | Resolution | "Here's what I actually recommend instead." | "Replace your calendar with a series backlog + Sunday review. Here's how." |

**Notes:**
- 3-4 installments typical
- The contrarian take should be defensible, not clickbait
- Counter-argument installment shows intellectual honesty
- Resolution pivots to practical guidance (bridge to mechanism arc)
- Works best as text posts on LinkedIn, opinion pieces in newsletter

## Installment design patterns

### Hook escalation

Later installments escalate engagement by referencing earlier ones:

| Installment | Hook Type | Example |
|-------------|----------|---------|
| #1 | Standalone hook | "Most B2B companies have GTM debt." |
| #2 | Callback hook | "Last week I named GTM debt. This week: where it hides." |
| #3 | Escalation hook | "In Parts 1-2, I covered messaging and positioning debt. Part 3 is the one that stings." |
| #4 | Resolution hook | "You've been waiting for the solution. Here it is." |

### Knowledge progression levels

| Level | Purpose | Content Depth | Audience Expectation |
|-------|---------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Awareness** | "I didn't know this was a problem" | Surface, provocative | Nothing — cold audience |
| **Understanding** | "Now I see how this works" | Conceptual, framework-level | Read the awareness piece |
| **Application** | "I can apply this myself" | Tactical, step-by-step | Understands the concept |
| **Mastery** | "I see how this connects to everything" | Synthesis, edge cases | Has tried applying it |

### Callback structure

Internal references that reward series followers:

- **Forward reference:** "Next week I'll show you exactly how to fix this."
- **Backward reference:** "Remember the positioning debt from Part 1? It compounds."
- **Cross-series reference:** "This connects to what I wrote in the 'Strategy That Ships' series."
- **Milestone reference:** "This is installment 10 of Operator Stories. Here's what the pattern shows."

## Funnel-stage format specifications

### TOFU formats (attract, build awareness)

| Format | Channel | Best For | Hook Style |
|--------|---------|----------|-----------|
| Personal stories | LinkedIn (text) | Problem arc, opinion arc | Vulnerable opening, lesson at end |
| Milestone posts | LinkedIn (text/photo) | Any arc (special installments) | Number-driven, achievement frame |
| Industry takes | LinkedIn (text), Newsletter | Opinion arc | Contrarian, data-backed |
| Hot takes | LinkedIn (text), X.com | Opinion arc | Short, punchy, polarizing |
| Trend commentary | LinkedIn (text), Newsletter | Any arc | "Here's what [trend] means for [audience]" |

### MOFU formats (nurture, demonstrate expertise)

| Format | Channel | Best For | Hook Style |
|--------|---------|----------|-----------|
| Resource giveaways | LinkedIn (document), Newsletter | Framework arc | "The template I use for [thing]" |
| Workflow breakdowns | LinkedIn (carousel), YouTube | Mechanism arc | "Step-by-step: how I [process]" |
| Carousel deep-dives | LinkedIn (document) | Mechanism arc, framework arc | Visual progression, slide-by-slide learning |
| Process teardowns | LinkedIn (text), Newsletter | Mechanism arc | "Here's exactly how I did [specific project]" |
| Tool deep-dives | LinkedIn (text/video), YouTube | Any arc | "How I use [tool] for [use case]" |

### BOFU formats (convert, prove results)

| Format | Channel | Best For | Hook Style |
|--------|---------|----------|-----------|
| Feature demos | LinkedIn (video), YouTube | Product series | Before/after, speed demonstration |
| Case study media | LinkedIn (text/carousel), Newsletter | Case study arc | Results-first, then story |
| Product launches | LinkedIn (text/video), Newsletter, YouTube | Launch arc | Countdown, reveal, early access |
| Before/after results | LinkedIn (carousel), Newsletter | Case study arc | Numbers side-by-side |
| Comparison content | Newsletter, LinkedIn (carousel) | Framework arc | Your approach vs. alternative |

## Series tracking template

### Series tracker (per series)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Series name** | [Name] |
| **Arc type** | Problem / Mechanism / Case study / Framework / Opinion |
| **Pillar** | [Pillar name] |
| **Funnel stage** | TOFU / MOFU / BOFU |
| **Planned installments** | [Number] |
| **Published** | [Number] of [Total] |
| **Status** | Planned / In-progress / Published complete / Retired |
| **Avg TWE** | [Score] |
| **Best installment** | #[Number] — TWE [Score] |
| **Decision** | Expand / Continue / Pivot / Retire |

### Decision triggers

| TWE Range | Signal | Action |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| > 2.0 average | Over-performer | Expand: add installments, spin off sub-series |
| 1.0 - 2.0 average | Performing | Continue: complete planned installments |
| 0.5 - 1.0 average | Under-performing | Pivot: adjust hook style, try different format |
| < 0.5 average | Not resonating | Retire: stop series, reallocate to better-performing arc |
