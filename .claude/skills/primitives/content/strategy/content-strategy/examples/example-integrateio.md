# Content Strategy Example: Integrate.io

**Company:** Integrate.io
**Category:** Data Delivery Platform
**Strategy period:** August 2025 onward

---

## Context

This example demonstrates a content strategy for a B2B SaaS company engaged in **category creation** — defining a new market category ("Data Delivery Platform") and a new buyer role ("Data Operator"). This requires heavy educational content balanced with personal founder storytelling to build authority and trust.

### Content Flow — How We Talk About It

**Tension:** Create tension with legacy approaches
- "Still manually moving data between systems?"
- "Enterprise tools are oil tankers. You need a speedboat."

**Educate:** Introduce Data Delivery Platform concept
- What it is → How it compares to ETL/iPaaS → How to adopt it

**Activate:** Resources to implement
- Guides, tutorials, templates, calculators

**Proof points:** Guide decision to Integrate.io
- Case studies, ROI demonstrations, objection handling

## 3. Channel Strategy

### Channel Selection

#### Basic Channels

**1. Blog/Newsletter — "Data Delivery Digest"**
- **Format:** Weekly newsletter with podcast transcripts + expanded written content
- **Frequency:** 4 articles/month (weekly)
- **Topics:** Deep-dives, frameworks, case studies from podcast
- **Key metrics:** Subscribers, open rate
- **Tooling:** Substack

**2. LinkedIn — Amplification**
- **Format:** Text posts, carousels, vertical shorts, image posts
- **Frequency:** 5x per week (20 posts/month)
- **Focus:** Thought leadership, podcast clips, key insights
- **Key metrics:** Followers, engagement
- **Tooling:** Opus (clips + scheduling)

#### Bonus Channels

**3. YouTube Podcast — "The Data Operator Podcast" (Anchor Content)**
- **Format:** Interview + solo educational episodes, 30-45 min
- **Frequency:** Weekly recording (4/month)
- **Topics:** Customer stories, industry trends, tactical tutorials
- **Key metrics:** Subscribers, downloads, retention
- **Tooling:** Zoom (recording + streaming), YouTube + Substack (publishing)

### Anchor Content Definition

**Anchor format:** YouTube Podcast — "The Data Operator Podcast"

**Rationale:** Podcast is highest-value content that generates multiple derivative pieces. Founder conversations with customers and industry experts build authority while creating repurposable content.

### Content Cascade

```
1. YouTube Podcast (Weekly 30-45 min episode)
         ↓
2. Blog/Newsletter — "Data Delivery Digest" (transcript + expansion)
         ↓
3. LinkedIn posts (5+ posts per episode: quotes, clips, insights)
         ↓
4. Short-form video clips (for LinkedIn video, YouTube Shorts)
         ↓
5. Quote graphics, carousels (key takeaways)
```

**Multiplier:** 1 podcast episode → 8+ derivative pieces

## Why This Strategy Works

### For Category Creation

1. **Heavy educational mix (40%)** — Essential for new category adoption
2. **Role-centric positioning** — Data Operator becomes the hero, not the tool
3. **Framework introduction** — "Speedboat vs Oil Tanker" creates memorable category distinction

### For Pipeline Generation

1. **Podcast anchor** — Builds relationships with customers and prospects
2. **Customer success emphasis** — Social proof drives conversion
3. **Comparison content** — Captures bottom-funnel search intent

### For Content Efficiency

1. **Cascade model** — 1 podcast = 8+ pieces reduces production burden
2. **Recurring series** — Predictable content reduces planning overhead
3. **Batching** — Monday production for 2-week LinkedIn coverage

---

## Key Takeaways for Similar Strategies

1. **Category creation requires education-heavy mix** — 40%+ educational content
2. **Anchor content multiplies efficiency** — Choose one high-effort format that cascades
3. **Role-led positioning differentiates** — Make the buyer the hero, not the product
4. **First 90 days should arc** — Introduce → Educate → Prove
5. **Recurring series create predictability** — Easier to plan, easier to execute

### Anti-examples (what to avoid)

| Bad Pattern | Why It's Bad | Better Approach |
|-------------|--------------|-----------------|
| "Post regularly on LinkedIn" | No specifics | "5 LinkedIn posts/week: 2 educational, 1 personal, 2 promotional" |
