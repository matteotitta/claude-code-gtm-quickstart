# Content Pillars Guide

Detailed guidance on the four content pillars: Educational, Personal, Promotional, and Organizational.

---

## The Four Pillars Framework

```
EDUCATIONAL (40%)          PERSONAL (25%)
Authority & education      Trust & connection
         │                        │
         └──────────┬─────────────┘
                    │
            ┌───────┴───────┐
            │   AUDIENCE    │
            └───────┬───────┘
                    │
         ┌──────────┴─────────────┐
         │                        │
PROMOTIONAL (25%)         ORGANIZATIONAL (10%)
Demand & conversion       Culture & talent
```

---

## Pillar 1: Educational (40%)

### Purpose
Establish authority in your category. Educate the market on concepts, frameworks, and best practices. This is the foundation of thought leadership.

### When to Use More
- Category creation (new category, need to educate market)
- Technical/complex product (buyers need understanding)
- Long sales cycles (nurturing through education)
- Competitive differentiation through expertise

### When to Use Less
- Established category (market already educated)
- Simple/intuitive product
- Short sales cycles
- Heavy promotion periods (launches)

### Format Types

| Format | Description | Best For | Effort |
|--------|-------------|----------|--------|
| **How-to guides** | Step-by-step tutorials | Bottom-funnel, search | High |
| **Framework introductions** | Conceptual models | Thought leadership | High |
| **Comparison content** | X vs Y analysis | Search intent, decision stage | Medium |
| **Technical deep-dives** | In-depth technical content | Champion personas | High |
| **Industry trend analysis** | Market commentary | Awareness, positioning | Medium |
| **Best practice lists** | Curated recommendations | Engagement, shareability | Low |
| **Glossaries/definitions** | Term explanations | SEO, education | Medium |
| **Pattern libraries** | Repeatable frameworks | Practitioners | High |

### Example Topics by Persona

**For Champions (practitioners):**
- "Complete guide to [process] in 2025"
- "[Tool A] vs [Tool B]: Practitioner's comparison"
- "5 [category] patterns every [role] needs"

**For Economic Buyers:**
- "How [category] reduces [cost metric]"
- "ROI framework for [category] investment"
- "[Industry] trends every [exec role] should know"

### Quality Markers

✓ Teaches something actionable
✓ Based on real expertise/data
✓ Provides frameworks, not just opinions
✓ Cites sources or shows proof
✓ Could be bookmarked and referenced later

✗ Generic advice anyone could give
✗ Theoretical without practical application
✗ Missing specific examples
✗ No clear takeaway or next step

## Pillar 2: Personal (25%)

### Purpose
Build trust through authenticity. Share founder/team stories, experiences, and perspectives. Humanize the brand and create emotional connection.

### When to Use More
- Founder-led brand (personal brand drives company)
- Category creation (need to be "the face" of movement)
- Trust-dependent purchase (high stakes decisions)
- Differentiation through personality

### When to Use Less
- Enterprise sales (company brand > personal)
- Founder not available for content
- Regulated industries (compliance concerns)
- Multi-founder confusion

### Format Types

| Format | Description | Best For | Effort |
|--------|-------------|----------|--------|
| **Founder journey** | Origin story, milestones | Brand building | Medium |
| **Hot takes** | Contrarian opinions | Engagement, visibility | Low |
| **Lessons learned** | Failures and growth | Trust, authenticity | Medium |
| **Behind the scenes** | How we do things | Culture, transparency | Low |
| **Personal reflections** | Thoughts on industry | Connection | Low |
| **Vulnerable moments** | Challenges, struggles | Deep trust | Medium |
| **Conference takeaways** | Event learnings | Timeliness, network | Low |
| **"Day in the life"** | Daily routines | Relatability | Low |

### Tone Guidance

**Do:**
- First person ("I" not "we")
- Specific stories with details
- Admit uncertainty and mistakes
- Show personality quirks
- Express genuine opinions

**Don't:**
- Corporate speak
- Generic inspiration
- Humble-bragging
- Excessive positivity
- Invented stories

### Example Topics

**Founder Journey:**
- "Why I left [previous role] to start this"
- "The moment I knew this was the problem to solve"
- "What I wish I knew before starting [company]"

**Hot Takes:**
- "Unpopular opinion: [conventional wisdom] is wrong"
- "Why I disagree with [common practice]"
- "[Industry] isn't ready for this conversation"

**Lessons Learned:**
- "The mistake that almost killed our company"
- "What my first enterprise deal taught me"
- "3 years in: What I got wrong"

### Quality Markers

✓ Specific, not generic
✓ Includes real details (names, dates, numbers)
✓ Shows genuine emotion or opinion
✓ Passes the "100 Posts Test"
✓ Only the founder could have written this

✗ Could be written by anyone
✗ Generic motivational content
✗ No specific story or detail
✗ Reads like corporate PR

## Pillar 3: Promotional (25%)

### Purpose
Drive demand and conversions. Showcase customer success, product capabilities, and offers. This is direct business development content.

### When to Use More
- Product launch periods
- Quarter-end pipeline pushes
- Strong case study inventory
- Promotional campaigns

### When to Use Less
- Early brand building (before trust established)
- Low product differentiation
- Limited customer proof
- Audience fatigue from promotion

### Format Types

| Format | Description | Best For | Effort |
|--------|-------------|----------|--------|
| **Customer case studies** | Detailed success stories | Social proof | High |
| **Feature announcements** | New capability launches | Awareness | Medium |
| **Demo invitations** | Live product showcases | Bottom-funnel | Low |
| **ROI calculators** | Value quantification | Decision stage | High |
| **Free trial offers** | Lead magnets | Conversion | Low |
| **Webinar invitations** | Live educational events | Lead gen | Medium |
| **Partnership announcements** | Ecosystem news | Credibility | Low |
| **Customer quotes** | Short testimonials | Quick proof | Low |

### Case Study Best Practices

**Structure:**
1. **Context:** Who is the customer? What's their industry/size?
2. **Challenge:** What problem were they facing?
3. **Solution:** How did your product help?
4. **Results:** Quantified outcomes (numbers!)
5. **Quote:** Verbatim customer testimonial

**Metrics to include:**
- Time saved
- Cost reduced
- Revenue increased
- Efficiency gained
- Risk mitigated

### Promotional Without Being Salesy

**Do:**
- Lead with customer story, not product
- Use specific metrics, not vague claims
- Include real customer quotes
- Show the transformation, not just the feature
- Natural CTAs that match content context

**Don't:**
- "Check out our amazing product!"
- Generic claims without proof
- Fabricated testimonials
- Every post is a pitch
- Pushy CTAs

### Example Topics

**Case Studies:**
- "How [Customer] achieved [Result] with [Product]"
- "[Customer]'s journey from [Before] to [After]"
- "[Industry] leader [Customer] on choosing [Product]"

**Feature Announcements:**
- "Introducing [Feature]: [Benefit in customer terms]"
- "New: [Feature] helps [persona] [achieve goal]"
- "Now available: [Capability] for [use case]"

### Quality Markers

✓ Customer is the hero, not the product
✓ Specific metrics and outcomes
✓ Real customer quotes (verbatim)
✓ Shows transformation journey
✓ CTA feels natural, not forced

✗ Product is the hero
✗ Vague claims ("improved efficiency")
✗ No customer proof
✗ Every post is a pitch
✗ Aggressive CTAs

## Pillar 4: Organizational (10%)

### Purpose
Showcase company culture and vision. Attract talent, build employer brand, and humanize the organization. This is about the team, not the product.

### When to Use More
- Active hiring (need talent attraction)
- Culture differentiation matters
- Internal milestone moments
- Team expansion periods

### When to Use Less
- Small team (not much to show)
- Talent attraction not a priority
- Company in flux/turmoil
- Limited bandwidth

### Format Types

| Format | Description | Best For | Effort |
|--------|-------------|----------|--------|
| **Team spotlights** | Individual profiles | Employer brand | Medium |
| **Company milestones** | Growth celebrations | Credibility | Low |
| **Culture stories** | How we work | Talent attraction | Medium |
| **Product roadmap** | Vision sharing | Customer retention | Medium |
| **Values in action** | Living the values | Authenticity | Medium |
| **Office/remote life** | Day in the life | Culture peek | Low |
| **Hiring announcements** | Open roles | Recruiting | Low |
| **Customer advisory** | Feedback sharing | Transparency | Medium |

### Team Spotlight Structure

**Include:**
1. Name and role
2. Background/journey to company
3. What they work on
4. Why they chose this company
5. Personal touch (hobby, quirk)
6. Advice for similar roles

**Tone:**
- Authentic voice (sounds like them)
- Specific details
- Personality shows through

### Example Topics

**Team Spotlights:**
- "Meet [Name]: Our [Role] shares their journey"
- "From [Previous Role] to [Current Role]: [Name]'s story"
- "Day in the life of our [Role]"

**Culture Stories:**
- "How we [unique practice] at [Company]"
- "Why we're remote-first (and what that really means)"
- "The 'speedboat' mentality: How we ship fast"

**Milestones:**
- "[Metric] customers: Thank you!"
- "[X] years of [Company]: Lessons learned"
- "We just hit [milestone] — here's how"

### Quality Markers

✓ Specific to your company
✓ Shows real people, not just logos
✓ Culture feels authentic, not manufactured
✓ Would actually attract talent
✓ Demonstrates values, doesn't just state them

✗ Generic "we're hiring" posts
✗ Corporate stock photo energy
✗ Values without proof
✗ Boastful without substance

## Mixing the Pillars

### Standard Mix (Default)

| Pillar | % | Rationale |
|--------|---|-----------|
| Educational | 40% | Builds authority, attracts top-funnel |
| Personal | 25% | Builds trust, differentiates |
| Promotional | 25% | Drives pipeline, converts |
| Organizational | 10% | Attracts talent, humanizes |

### Adjusted Mixes

**Category Creation (Educate the market):**
| Pillar | % | Notes |
|--------|---|-------|
| Educational | 50% | Heavy education needed |
| Personal | 25% | Founder is category face |
| Promotional | 20% | Less push, more pull |
| Organizational | 5% | Lower priority |

**Product Launch Period:**
| Pillar | % | Notes |
|--------|---|-------|
| Educational | 30% | Reduced temporarily |
| Personal | 15% | Background role |
| Promotional | 50% | Launch push |
| Organizational | 5% | Minimal |

**Hiring Push:**
| Pillar | % | Notes |
|--------|---|-------|
| Educational | 30% | Maintained for authority |
| Personal | 25% | Attracts culture-fit |
| Promotional | 20% | Reduced |
| Organizational | 25% | Increased for talent |

## Content Flow Within Pillars

### Tension → Educate → Activate → Proof

**Educational content follows:**
```
TENSION:   "Why the old way doesn't work"
EDUCATE:   "Here's the new framework"
ACTIVATE:  "How to implement it"
```

**Personal content follows:**
```
TENSION:   "The challenge I faced"
EDUCATE:   "What I learned from it"
ACTIVATE:  "What you can apply"
```

**Promotional content follows:**
```
TENSION:   "The problem customer had"
EDUCATE:   "How they solved it"
PROOF:     "The results they achieved"
```

## Pillar Selection Checklist

Before creating content, ask:

1. **What's the primary goal?**
   - Build authority → Educational
   - Build trust → Personal
   - Drive conversion → Promotional
   - Attract talent → Organizational

2. **Who's the primary audience?**
   - Prospects learning → Educational
   - Network building → Personal
   - Buyers evaluating → Promotional
   - Candidates researching → Organizational

3. **What do we have to work with?**
   - Expertise/frameworks → Educational
   - Founder stories → Personal
   - Customer success → Promotional
   - Team culture → Organizational

4. **What's the current mix?**
   - Too much promo? Add educational
   - Too impersonal? Add personal
   - Not converting? Add promotional
   - Hiring struggling? Add organizational
