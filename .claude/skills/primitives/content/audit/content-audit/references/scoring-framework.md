# Content Audit Scoring Framework

Detailed rubrics for consistent scoring across all audit dimensions.

---

## Overall Scoring Scale

| Score | Label | Definition |
|-------|-------|------------|
| **5** | Excellent | Best-in-class, exceeds B2B benchmarks, few/no gaps |
| **4** | Strong | Above average, meets most benchmarks, minor improvements |
| **3** | Adequate | Meets baseline expectations, clear areas for improvement |
| **2** | Weak | Below average, significant gaps, needs attention |
| **1** | Critical | Major issues, requires immediate intervention |

---

## 1. Volume Score

**What it measures:** Total content output across all channels relative to B2B benchmarks.

### Scoring Rubric

| Score | Newsletter/Blog | LinkedIn | YouTube | Events | Total/Month |
|-------|-----------------|----------|---------|--------|-------------|
| **5** | 8+/month | Daily (20+/month) | Weekly (4+/month) | 2+/month | 35+ pieces |
| **4** | 4-7/month | 15-19/month | 2-3/month | 1/month | 25-34 pieces |
| **3** | 2-3/month | 10-14/month | 1/month | 1/quarter | 15-24 pieces |
| **2** | 1/month | 5-9/month | <1/month | <1/quarter | 8-14 pieces |
| **1** | <1/month | <5/month | None | None | <8 pieces |

### Scoring Guidelines

- **Weight by channel importance:** LinkedIn and Newsletter typically matter most for B2B
- **Consider company stage:** Seed startups have lower expectations than Series B+
- **Factor team size:** Solo founder vs. content team affects expectations

### Volume Score Examples

**Score 5 example:**
> "Publishing 6 newsletter issues, 25 LinkedIn posts, 4 YouTube videos, and 2 webinars monthly. Total: 37 pieces/month — exceeds all benchmarks."

**Score 3 example:**
> "Publishing 2 newsletter issues and 12 LinkedIn posts monthly. No video or events. Total: 14 pieces/month — meets baseline but gaps in video/events."

**Score 1 example:**
> "Last newsletter was 3 months ago. LinkedIn has 3 posts this month. No other channels active. Total: ~4 pieces/month — critical gap."

## 2. Consistency Score

**What it measures:** Publishing regularity and predictability across channels.

### Scoring Rubric

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **5** | Predictable schedule across all channels, no gaps >7 days on LinkedIn, no gaps >14 days on newsletter |
| **4** | Mostly consistent, occasional minor gaps, clear patterns |
| **3** | Generally regular but unpredictable gaps, no established rhythm |
| **2** | Irregular posting, frequent long gaps, stop-start patterns |
| **1** | Sporadic/abandoned, gaps >30 days common, no discernible pattern |

### Consistency Indicators

**Positive signals:**
- Same publishing day each week (e.g., "Newsletter every Tuesday")
- Regular posting times
- No unexplained gaps
- Maintained schedule during holidays (or planned breaks)

**Negative signals:**
- Random posting days
- Multi-week gaps without explanation
- Burst-and-fade patterns
- Abandoned channels (no posts in 30+ days)

### Consistency Score Examples

**Score 5 example:**
> "Newsletter publishes every Tuesday at 9am ET. LinkedIn posts daily on weekdays. YouTube uploads every Wednesday. No gaps longer than planned holidays."

**Score 3 example:**
> "Newsletter averages weekly but varies between 5-12 days. LinkedIn posts 3-5x/week but timing varies. YouTube uploads monthly but no fixed day."

**Score 1 example:**
> "Newsletter had 3 issues in January, 0 in February, 2 in March. LinkedIn goes silent for 10-15 days regularly. Last YouTube video was 4 months ago."

## 3. Quality Score

**What it measures:** Content depth, production value, and professional standards.

### Scoring Rubric

| Score | Newsletter/Blog | LinkedIn | YouTube |
|-------|-----------------|----------|---------|
| **5** | Deep insights, unique frameworks, excellent writing, professional visuals, strong CTAs | Compelling hooks, substantive content, high engagement, distinctive voice | Professional production, clear audio/video, strong editing, optimized titles/thumbnails |
| **4** | Good depth, clear writing, relevant visuals, actionable content | Good hooks, valuable content, above-average engagement | Good production quality, clear delivery, decent optimization |
| **3** | Adequate depth, acceptable writing, basic visuals | Standard posts, moderate engagement, generic hooks | Basic production, understandable quality, generic thumbnails |
| **2** | Shallow content, unclear writing, missing visuals, weak structure | Poor hooks, low engagement, generic content | Poor audio/video quality, unedited, no optimization |
| **1** | Minimal effort, errors, no value-add, missing elements | Spammy, no engagement, purely promotional | Unwatchable quality, no editing, misleading titles |

### Quality Indicators by Channel

**Newsletter/Blog quality signals:**
| Element | High Quality | Low Quality |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Depth | 1500+ words with frameworks | <500 words, surface-level |
| Structure | Clear headers, scannable | Wall of text |
| Visuals | Custom graphics, charts | Stock photos or none |
| CTAs | Clear, relevant | Missing or generic |
| Originality | Unique insights | Rehashed content |

**LinkedIn quality signals:**
| Element | High Quality | Low Quality |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Hook | Compelling first line | Generic opener |
| Value | Actionable insights | Vague platitudes |
| Format | Appropriate for content | Wrong format choice |
| Voice | Distinctive, authentic | Generic, corporate |
| Engagement | Comments with substance | Low/no engagement |

**YouTube quality signals:**
| Element | High Quality | Low Quality |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Audio | Clear, no background noise | Echo, muffled, noise |
| Video | Good lighting, stable | Dark, shaky, blurry |
| Editing | Tight cuts, graphics | Raw footage, no cuts |
| Thumbnails | Custom, branded | Auto-generated |
| Titles | Keyword-optimized | Generic or clickbait |

### Quality Score Examples

**Score 5 example:**
> "Newsletter features original frameworks with detailed explanations, custom diagrams, and specific examples. Average 2000 words with clear structure. LinkedIn posts have 3-5% engagement with substantive comment threads. YouTube has professional audio, custom thumbnails, and clear editing."

**Score 3 example:**
> "Newsletter is readable but lacks depth — mostly industry news summaries. LinkedIn posts are standard text posts with 1-2% engagement. YouTube is shot on webcam with okay audio but no editing or custom thumbnails."

## 4. Pillar Balance Score

**What it measures:** Distribution of content across Educational, Personal, Promotional, and Organizational pillars.

### Target Mix (B2B Standard)

| Pillar | Target % | Acceptable Range |
|--------|----------|------------------|
| Educational | 40% | 35-50% |
| Personal | 25% | 15-30% |
| Promotional | 25% | 15-30% |
| Organizational | 10% | 5-15% |

### Scoring Rubric

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **5** | All pillars within acceptable range, strategic balance intentional |
| **4** | Minor imbalance (one pillar off by 5-10%), otherwise healthy |
| **3** | One pillar significantly over/underrepresented (10-20% off target) |
| **2** | Two or more pillars significantly imbalanced, or one pillar missing entirely |
| **1** | Extreme imbalance (e.g., 90% promotional) or only one pillar present |

### Common Imbalance Patterns

**Over-educational (common):**
> 70% educational, 10% personal, 15% promotional, 5% organizational
> *Issue: Not building personal connection or driving conversions*

**Over-promotional (common):**
> 20% educational, 5% personal, 70% promotional, 5% organizational
> *Issue: Appears salesy, not building trust*

**Missing personal (common):**
> 50% educational, 0% personal, 40% promotional, 10% organizational
> *Issue: No human connection, feels corporate*

### Pillar Balance Examples

**Score 5 example:**
> "Educational 42%, Personal 23%, Promotional 26%, Organizational 9%. All pillars within target range. Good variety across all content types."

**Score 3 example:**
> "Educational 55%, Personal 10%, Promotional 30%, Organizational 5%. Over-indexed on educational, under-indexed on personal content. Strategy feels informational but lacks human connection."

**Score 1 example:**
> "Promotional 85%, Educational 15%. No personal or organizational content at all. Every post is about the product — feels spammy."

## 5. Cross-Channel Alignment Score

**What it measures:** Consistency of voice, topics, and brand across channels, plus content repurposing efficiency.

### Scoring Rubric

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **5** | Consistent voice across all channels, topics align, active cross-promotion, efficient content cascade (anchor → derivatives), unified brand experience |
| **4** | Mostly consistent, minor voice variations appropriate to channel, some cross-promotion, some repurposing |
| **3** | Recognizably same brand but inconsistent execution, minimal cross-promotion, limited repurposing |
| **2** | Noticeably different voice/topics per channel, no cross-promotion, no repurposing, feels fragmented |
| **1** | Channels feel like different brands, contradictory messaging, isolated content silos |

### Alignment Indicators

**Voice consistency:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| High | Same person/tone recognizable across channels |
| Medium | Similar but noticeable adjustments |
| Low | Different voices, feels like different authors |

**Topic alignment:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| High | Core topics appear across all channels |
| Medium | Some overlap but channel-specific focus |
| Low | Different topics per channel, no cohesion |

**Cross-promotion:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| High | Regular mentions of other channels, links, CTAs |
| Medium | Occasional cross-references |
| Low | Channels operate independently |

**Content cascade:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| High | Anchor content systematically repurposed to 5+ derivatives |
| Medium | Some repurposing but not systematic |
| Low | Each channel has unique content, no repurposing |

### Cross-Channel Examples

**Score 5 example:**
> "Podcast episodes (anchor) are repurposed into newsletter issues, 5-8 LinkedIn posts per episode, YouTube clips, and quote graphics. Consistent founder voice across all channels. Newsletter promotes podcast, LinkedIn promotes newsletter, creating clear content funnel."

**Score 3 example:**
> "Newsletter and LinkedIn cover similar topics but voice is more formal in newsletter. YouTube feels separate — different topics, different style. Occasional cross-promotion but not systematic. Some content could be repurposed but isn't."

**Score 1 example:**
> "Newsletter is technical deep-dives, LinkedIn is inspirational quotes, YouTube is product demos. No connection between channels. Voice varies wildly. No cross-promotion. Feels like three different companies."

## 6. Overall Health Score

**Calculation:** Weighted average of all dimension scores.

### Weighting (Default B2B)

| Dimension | Weight | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Volume | 20% | Foundation — can't optimize what doesn't exist |
| Consistency | 20% | Trust-building requires reliability |
| Quality | 25% | Quality > quantity for B2B |
| Pillar Balance | 20% | Strategic mix drives results |
| Cross-Channel | 15% | Efficiency and brand cohesion |

### Overall Score Calculation

```
Overall = (Volume × 0.20) + (Consistency × 0.20) + (Quality × 0.25) +
          (Pillar Balance × 0.20) + (Cross-Channel × 0.15)
```

### Overall Score Interpretation

| Score | Label | Interpretation |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| 4.5-5.0 | Excellent | Content program is a competitive advantage |
| 3.5-4.4 | Strong | Solid foundation with room to optimize |
| 2.5-3.4 | Adequate | Functional but significant improvements possible |
| 1.5-2.4 | Weak | Content is not driving results, needs strategic overhaul |
| 1.0-1.4 | Critical | Content program requires immediate intervention |

## 7. Scoring Adjustments

### Company Stage Adjustments

| Stage | Volume Adjustment | Quality Adjustment |
|-------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Pre-seed/Seed | -1 to volume expectations | -0.5 to quality expectations |
| Series A | Standard expectations | Standard expectations |
| Series B+ | +0.5 to all expectations | +0.5 to all expectations |

### Team Size Adjustments

| Team | Adjustment |
|------|------------|
| Solo founder | -1 to volume, -0.5 to consistency |
| 1 content person | Standard expectations |
| Content team (2+) | +0.5 to all expectations |

### Industry Adjustments

| Industry | Adjustment |
|----------|------------|
| Developer tools | +0.5 to quality expectations for technical depth |
| Enterprise B2B | Lower volume acceptable if quality high |
| SMB B2B | Higher volume expected for category education |

## 8. Scoring Documentation

When assigning scores, document:

```markdown
### [Dimension] Score: [X]/5

**Rating:** [Label]

**Key findings:**
- [Finding 1]
- [Finding 2]
- [Finding 3]

**Score rationale:**
[1-2 sentences explaining why this score was assigned]

**Improvement path to [X+1]:**
[What would move score up by 1 point]
```

### Example Documented Score

```markdown
### Volume Score: 3/5

**Rating:** Adequate

**Key findings:**
- Newsletter: 2.5 posts/month (below 4/month benchmark)
- LinkedIn: 12 posts/month (meets 10-14 benchmark)
- YouTube: None
- Events: 1/quarter (meets minimum)

**Score rationale:**
Meets baseline on LinkedIn and events but below benchmark on newsletter and missing video entirely. Total ~15 pieces/month is adequate but not competitive.

**Improvement path to 4:**
Increase newsletter to 4/month and add at least 1 YouTube video/month. Would bring total to ~20 pieces/month.
```
